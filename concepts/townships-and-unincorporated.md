# Townships: no municipal income tax (usually)

Ohio **townships do not levy income tax**. The Ohio Township Association states it directly:
"Townships do not levy income or sales taxes." Structurally, no provision of the Revised Code
grants townships income-tax power — ORC Chapter 718 empowers *municipal corporations* (cities
and villages) only.

**Consequence for refunds:** a township resident has **no residence municipal income tax** and
therefore no residence-side credit math. If their employer withholds city tax for an office they
didn't actually work in, a refund on [Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-wfh-refund.md) is *pure
recovery* — no residence city claws any of it back (compare
[Credit for taxes paid](credit-for-taxes-paid.md), where a residence city usually absorbs part of
the refund). WFH days worked in a township are taxed by no municipality at all (2022+ rules).

**The exception — JEDDs and JEDZs:** a township may partner with a municipality in a Joint
Economic Development District (ORC 715.70–715.72), whose board may levy an income tax **within
the district's territory** (typically a commercial corridor) — "a revenue source that would
otherwise be unavailable to the township." Many appear in RITA's rates table (e.g., the Medina
Montville JEDD, RITA code 149, 1.25%). Working *inside* a JEDD zone means that JEDD's tax
applies even though the surrounding township levies none. The Ohio Department of Taxation's
"Finder" tool looks up JEDD/JEDZ rates by address.

Example: Hinckley Township (Medina County) appears nowhere in RITA's 2026 table — its residents
owe no residence municipal income tax.
