# Statute of limitations for municipal tax refunds

**The rule** — ORC 718.19(B)(1) (current version, eff. 2025-09-30): refund requests
"shall be filed with the tax administrator, on the form prescribed by the tax administrator
**within three years after the tax return, including any valid extension, was due or paid,
whichever is later.**"

For a W-2 employee whose tax was paid via withholding during the year, the later date is normally
the **return due date** — so the practical deadline is **three years from the (extended) filing
deadline for that tax year**. RITA's own instructions state it the same way, with this example:
"Tax year 2025 filing deadline is 04/15/2026, therefore a request for refund must be received by
04/15/2029."

Related rules:

- ORC 718.12(C) confirms refund claims follow 718.19's clock; ORC 718.12(D) provides interest on
  overpayments unless refunded within 90 days.
- RITA's Rules & Regulations §9 phrase the window as "within three years after the tax was due or
  paid, whichever is later."
- Requests past the window "will be denied" (RITA 10A instructions). A late claim has no appeal
  path around the statute.
