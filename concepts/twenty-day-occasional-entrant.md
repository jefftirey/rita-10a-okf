# The 20-day occasional entrant rule

ORC 718.011(B)(1): an employer is **not required to withhold** municipal income tax on wages for
services an employee performed in a municipality on **twenty or fewer days in a calendar year** —
unless one of these applies:

- the municipality is the employee's **principal place of work**;
- the employee worked at a **presumed worksite location** there (e.g., a construction site); or
- the employee is a **resident** of that municipality.

Once an employee passes 20 days in a city, withholding is required there for **subsequent days**
that year (ORC 718.011(D)(1)); the employer may elect to withhold retroactively for the first
20 days (D)(2).

**"Principal place of work"** (ORC 718.011(A)(7)) is "the fixed location to which an employee is
required to report for employment duties on a regular and ordinary basis," with statutory
fallbacks if there is none.

Notes for consuming agents:

- This is a **withholding** rule for employers. The employee's actual liability follows where
  work was performed — an occasional-entrant day can still create employee-side tax in that city
  once thresholds are passed; RITA's Form 10A worksheet warns that days worked in another taxing
  municipality are "subject to tax in that municipality."
- The rule was suspended in practice during 2020–2021 by the pandemic legislation — see
  [Work-from-home rules by year](work-from-home-rules-by-year.md). It has been back in ordinary
  operation since January 1, 2022.
