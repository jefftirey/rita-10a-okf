# Open claim windows

**This node is a living fact.** It applies the 3-year
[statute of limitations](statute-of-limitations.md) to each tax year and is regenerated as
deadlines pass. It was last verified **2026-07-14**; if you are reading it much later,
recompute from the rule before relying on the table.

| Tax year | Return due | Claim deadline | Status as of 2026-07-14 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2020 | 2021-05-17 (COVID-extended) | ~May 2024 | **Closed** — and [Schaad](../law/schaad-v-alder.md) bars Ohio residents' WFH claims on the merits anyway |
| 2021 | 2022-04-18 | ~Apr 2025 | **Closed** |
| 2022 | 2023-04-18 | ~Apr 2026 | **Closed** for most; a filer with a valid extension (return due ~2023-10-16) is open until **~2026-10-16** |
| 2023 | 2024-04-15 | ~2027-04-15 | **Open** |
| 2024 | 2025-04-15 | ~2028-04-15 | **Open** |
| 2025 | 2026-04-15 | ~2029-04-15 | **Open** |
| 2026 | 2027-04-15 | ~2030-04-15 | **Not yet claimable** — year in progress; Form 10A is filed per W-2 after year-end. Over-withholding is better fixed prospectively with the employer |

Notes:

- There is **no end date to the refund right itself** — it is permanent law (ORC 718.19), not a
  pandemic program. Each new tax year opens a new 3-year window when it ends.
- Historical due dates above (2021-05-17, 2022-04-18, 2023-04-18) are the standard
  federal/Ohio deadlines of those years; only the 2022 boundary is sensitive to day-level precision.
- A consuming agent should check this node **before** walking a person through any refund
  procedure — a perfect claim for a closed year is worth nothing.
