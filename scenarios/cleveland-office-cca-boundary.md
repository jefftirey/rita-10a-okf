# RITA suburb resident, Cleveland office: the two-agency case

**Situation.** A resident of a RITA suburb — say
[North Royalton](../municipalities/north-royalton/index.md) — commutes (or used to) to an office in
downtown **Cleveland**, working part of the year from home. Cleveland withholds its 2.50% all year.

**The catch:** Cleveland's income tax is administered by **CCA (Central Collection Agency)**,
not RITA — see [RITA vs. CCA vs. self-administered](../concepts/rita-vs-cca-vs-self-administered.md).
RITA's Form 10A **cannot refund Cleveland tax.** RITA says so directly: it cannot refund tax
withheld for a non-RITA municipality.

**How the pieces split:**

- **Workplace side (Cleveland → CCA):** the WFH-day refund claim for an open year
  ([2023–2025](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md)) is made under **CCA's own refund process** on
  CCA's forms (ccaohio.gov). The same substantive law applies — days worked at home aren't
  Cleveland days under [ordinary ORC 718](../concepts/work-from-home-rules-by-year.md) — but the
  paperwork and agency are different. This bundle documents the boundary, not CCA's procedure.
- **Residence side (suburb → RITA):** a granted Cleveland refund shrinks the residence credit
  for taxes paid to Cleveland, and the RITA residence return must reflect it
  ([amended returns](../procedures/amended-returns.md)). Whether the refund nets anything
  depends on the suburb's [credit](../concepts/credit-for-taxes-paid.md) — e.g. North Royalton
  credits 100% but only up to 1.25%, well under Cleveland's 2.50%, so its residents pay a spread
  both ways and a WFH refund has real value.
- **WFH days worked in the home suburb** are that suburb's taxable days (or nobody's, in a
  [township](../concepts/townships-and-unincorporated.md)).

**Agent checklist for two-agency cases:** identify each municipality's administrator first;
route each claim to the right agency; compute the net across both sides before quoting a number;
cite each agency's own pages.
