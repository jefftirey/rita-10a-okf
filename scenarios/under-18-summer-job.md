# Under-18 with a summer job: the age exemption refund

**Situation.** A 16-year-old works a summer job in a RITA municipality; the employer's payroll
withholds municipal income tax like anyone else's. That money is claimable.

**The rule.** Per RITA's instructions: **"Beginning with tax year 2024, all municipalities
exempt income earned under the age of 18."** For tax years before 2024, exemption ages varied by
municipality — for an older open year, check the specific municipality's rules ("Special Notes"
on its RITA profile page) before assuming.

**The claim** ([Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-overview.md), claim reason 1 — Age Exemption):

- Attach the W-2 and **proof of birthdate**.
- No [employer certification/contact](../procedures/employer-certification.md) is required for
  this reason.
- The usual mechanics apply: paper filing, one form per W-2/municipality/year, refunds over $10
  only, ~90-day processing, and the 3-year
  [claim window](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md) (a 2024 summer job is claimable until
  ~April 2028 — teenagers have time, but the money doesn't come unless someone files).

**Why this scenario earns its place:** it's the simplest possible 10A — no allocation worksheet,
no employer involvement, no residence-side clawback complexity for most teens — and it's the
kind of small, uncontested refund that goes unclaimed because nobody knows the form exists.
