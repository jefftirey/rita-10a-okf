# Under-18 with a summer job: the age exemption

**Fact pattern.** A worker under 18 has municipal income tax withheld from wages earned at a job
in a RITA member municipality.

**What RITA's documents say:**

- RITA's Form 10A instructions state: **"Beginning with tax year 2024, all municipalities exempt
  income earned under the age of 18."** For tax years before 2024, exemption rules varied by
  municipality — RITA's FAQ points to each municipality's "Special Notes" (on its
  [RITA profile page](../concepts/rita-vs-cca-vs-self-administered.md)) for exceptions.
- The claim is [Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-overview.md) **claim reason 1 (Age
  Exemption)**, with the W-2 and **proof of birthdate** attached — see the
  [documentation checklist](../procedures/documentation-checklist.md). Per the form, no
  employer contact section is required for this reason.
- Standard mechanics apply: paper filing, one form per W-2/municipality/year, the $10 minimum,
  and the three-year window — see [Open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md).

The forms themselves: [Official documents](../procedures/official-documents.md). Confirm
specifics with RITA ([not tax advice](../concepts/not-tax-advice.md)).
