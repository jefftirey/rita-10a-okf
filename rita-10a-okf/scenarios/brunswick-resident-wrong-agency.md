# Brunswick resident: a self-administered city

**Fact pattern.** A Brunswick resident asks about municipal tax filings or refunds. Brunswick
sits among RITA suburbs (neighboring Medina *is* a RITA member), which invites the assumption
that Brunswick is RITA too.

**What the record shows:**

- **Brunswick is not a RITA member.** It operates its own Income Tax Department
  (brunswick.oh.us/income-tax; the city's page states a 2.0% rate and a credit of up to 1.0%).
  Brunswick appears in neither RITA's nor CCA's member lists.
- **Brunswick-side filings** (returns, credits, refunds of Brunswick tax) therefore go to
  Brunswick's own tax department, on Brunswick's forms.
- **Workplace-side claims follow the workplace city's administrator.** If a Brunswick
  resident's employer withheld tax for a RITA member city (for example
  [Strongsville](../municipalities/strongsville/index.md)) for days not actually worked there,
  the claim against that city's withholding is RITA's
  [Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-overview.md). RITA's instructions address non-RITA
  residents: a RITA return may be created to report work performed in RITA municipalities.
- The general pattern is documented in
  [RITA vs. CCA vs. self-administered](../concepts/rita-vs-cca-vs-self-administered.md); the
  same applies to Parma, Lakewood, and Fairlawn residents.

Confirm with the specific city's tax department and RITA
([not tax advice](../concepts/not-tax-advice.md)).
