# Work-from-home in 2020 or 2021: the closed years

**Fact pattern.** An employee worked from home during 2020 and/or 2021 while the employer
withheld tax for the office municipality, and asks about refunds now (mid-2026).

**What the record shows:**

- **Tax year 2020:** [HB 197 §29](../law/hb-197-2020.md) deemed pandemic WFH days to be days at
  the principal place of work. The Ohio Supreme Court upheld it in
  [Schaad v. Alder, 2024-Ohio-525](../law/schaad-v-alder.md) (decided 2024-02-14) — read the
  opinion for the holding. Separately, the three-year window of
  [ORC 718.19(B)(1)](../law/orc-718.md) ran out for 2020 in 2024. RITA's FAQ described timely
  2020 claims as held "SUSPENDED" pending the litigation. (The *Morsy v. Dumas* case page
  documents a different outcome for an out-of-state plaintiff — see the
  [Schaad node](../law/schaad-v-alder.md).)
- **Tax year 2021:** [HB 110](../law/hb-110-2021.md) permitted employee refund claims for days
  not actually worked in the withholding municipality (see the LSC language quoted in that
  node). The three-year window for 2021 ran out in April 2025.
- **Both years** are therefore outside the current
  [open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md); RITA's instructions state requests
  past the limitation "will be denied."

**Where the live question usually is:** if the same work pattern continued, tax years 2023–2025
remain within the window — see [Open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md) and the
[2024 hybrid scenario](wfh-2024-medina-resident-solon-office.md). Confirm any specific situation
with RITA or a preparer ([not tax advice](../concepts/not-tax-advice.md)).
