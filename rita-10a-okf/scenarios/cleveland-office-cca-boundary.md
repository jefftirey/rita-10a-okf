# RITA suburb resident, Cleveland office: the two-agency case

**Fact pattern.** A resident of a RITA member suburb (for example
[North Royalton](../municipalities/north-royalton/index.md)) works at an office in **Cleveland**,
some days from home, and Cleveland tax was withheld all year.

**What applies:**

- **Cleveland is not RITA.** Cleveland's income tax is administered by **CCA (Central
  Collection Agency)** — the City of Cleveland's own Division of Taxation page says CCA's
  purpose is "to collect municipal income tax for the City of Cleveland." See
  [RITA vs. CCA vs. self-administered](../concepts/rita-vs-cca-vs-self-administered.md).
- **RITA states it cannot refund tax withheld for a non-RITA municipality** — so a refund claim
  for the Cleveland withholding follows **CCA's process and forms** (ccaohio.gov), not RITA's
  Form 10A. This bundle documents the boundary; CCA's procedures are on CCA's site.
- **The residence side stays with RITA:** the suburb's return, its credit for taxes paid to
  Cleveland, and any amendment after a refund run through RITA — see
  [Amended returns](../procedures/amended-returns.md) and the suburb's own
  [rates and credits](../municipalities/north-royalton/rates-and-credits.md).
- Days actually worked at home are governed by the ordinary rules for the year — see
  [Work-from-home rules by year](../concepts/work-from-home-rules-by-year.md) and
  [Open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md).

Confirm the two-agency mechanics with CCA and RITA directly, or with a preparer
([not tax advice](../concepts/not-tax-advice.md)).
