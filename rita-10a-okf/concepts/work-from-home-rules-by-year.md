# Work-from-home rules by tax year

Which municipality can tax a day you worked at home depends entirely on the **tax year**. Three
regimes exist. Before answering any WFH question, identify the year, then check
[Open claim windows](open-claim-windows.md) — as of mid-2026, only 2023 and later are claimable.

## Through 2019, and again from 2022 onward — ordinary law

A day worked at home is a day worked in the *home* municipality (or township). The employer
withholds for where work is physically performed (ORC 718.03), subject to the
[20-day occasional entrant rule](twenty-day-occasional-entrant.md) (ORC 718.011). If the
employer withheld to the office city anyway, the employee may request a refund for those days on
[Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-wfh-refund.md), claim reason "Days Worked From Home." The
"traditional 20-day rule was reinstated" effective January 1, 2022 (McDonald Hopkins, citing
HB 110's expiration).

## Tax year 2020 — HB 197 §29: WFH days deemed office days, and it stuck

Ohio's pandemic law ([HB 197, 2020](../law/hb-197-2020.md)) deemed any day worked from home
"because of the declaration" to be a day worked at the employee's **principal place of work** —
so office-city withholding continued and was *legally correct*. The Ohio Supreme Court upheld
this in [Schaad v. Alder](../law/schaad-v-alder.md) (2024-Ohio-525, decided 2024-02-14):
**Ohio residents have no 2020 WFH refund right.** Exception: out-of-state residents (see
*Morsy v. Dumas* in the Schaad node) — Ohio cannot tax days worked entirely outside Ohio.
Either way, the [claim window](open-claim-windows.md) for 2020 closed in 2024.

## Tax year 2021 — HB 110: employers keep withholding, employees may claim refunds

[HB 110](../law/hb-110-2021.md) extended the office-city *withholding* rule through
2021-12-31 but made it withholding-only: on and after 2021-01-01 it did not determine the
*employee's* liability. Per the Legislative Service Commission, municipalities were required
"to approve a nonresident employee's request for a refund of taxes withheld on and after
January 1, 2021... provided the income was earned while the employee was not actually working in
that municipality." So 2021 WFH refunds were legitimate — but the
[claim window](open-claim-windows.md) closed in April 2025.

## Summary table

| Tax years | Governing law | WFH day taxed by | Refund for WFH days? |
|---|---|---|---|
| ≤ 2019 | ORC 718 | home location | Yes (window now closed) |
| 2020 | HB 197 §29, upheld by *Schaad* | office city (deemed) | **No** for Ohio residents; window closed regardless |
| 2021 | HB 110 | home location (withholding excepted) | Yes — window closed April 2025 |
| 2022+ | ORC 718 | home location | **Yes — 2023, 2024, 2025 open as of mid-2026** |
