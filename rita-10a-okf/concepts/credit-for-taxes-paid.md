# Credit for taxes paid to another municipality

Most residence municipalities grant a credit for income tax paid to a workplace city. RITA's
Tax Rates Table publishes two numbers per member municipality that govern it:

- **Credit factor** (shown as "Credit Factor (Tax Credit)") — the percentage of workplace tax
  credited.
- **Credit limit** (shown as "Credit Rate (Credit Limit)") — the rate ceiling the credit
  applies up to.

**This bundle does not compute credits.** The computation is defined by each municipality's own
ordinance (linked from its RITA profile page) and applied through RITA's Form 37 (the annual
return) and its instructions. To evaluate any specific situation, retrieve:

1. Both municipalities' rate, credit factor, and credit limit **for the tax year in question** —
   the [Tax Rates Table](https://www.ritaohio.com/TaxRatesTable) has year tabs (2020–2026), and
   each city's profile page shows its "Recent Tax and Credit History." The per-city nodes in
   [municipalities/](../municipalities/index.md) carry the verified 2026 values and the profile links.
2. The residence municipality's ordinance (profile page).
3. RITA's Form 37 instructions (via the [forms index](../procedures/official-documents.md)).

**Why it matters to refunds:** RITA's own instructions state that residence returns "will be
amended to disallow any residence tax credit taken for amounts refunded with this Form 10A,"
and its FAQ states that after a workplace refund "you will likely owe that tax, or some portion
of it, to your residence community." So the residence city's credit terms determine the net
outcome of any [Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-overview.md) refund — which is why both
municipalities' published numbers, for the right year, must be in hand before anyone quotes a
result. See [Residence vs. workplace tax](residence-vs-workplace-tax.md) and
[Amended returns](../procedures/amended-returns.md).
