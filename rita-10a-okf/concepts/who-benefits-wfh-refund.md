# Who benefits most from a work-from-home refund

A Form 10A refund of workplace-city tax comes with a documented counterweight: RITA amends the
residence return to disallow the credit taken on refunded amounts, and per RITA's FAQ "you will
likely owe that tax, or some portion of it, to your residence community." How much "some
portion" is depends entirely on **where the claimant lives**. The published credit terms imply a
directional ranking — **directional only: the exact computation is defined by each
municipality's ordinance and RITA's Form 37 instructions, and this bundle does not compute
individual outcomes.**

1. **No residence municipal tax at all — the maximum-benefit case.** Residents of
   [townships and unincorporated areas](townships-and-unincorporated.md) (e.g., Hinckley
   Township) owe no residence municipal income tax, so there is no credit to disallow and no
   residence-side tax on the home-worked wages: the refund is **pure recovery**. Note the cut
   is "no municipal tax," not "outside RITA" — a resident of a self-administered *city* (e.g.,
   Brunswick, 2.0%) still owes residence tax at home.
2. **Residence city with a low or zero credit factor.** Where little or no credit was being
   taken (RITA's 2026 table lists members with 0% credit factors, e.g., South Euclid or East
   Cleveland), there is little or nothing to disallow — the refund is kept nearly in full,
   though the resident's own city tax on those wages continues as it always did.
3. **Partial-credit residence city.** Part of the refund is offset by the disallowed credit.
   *Illustrative:* a [Strongsville](../municipalities/strongsville/index.md) resident (75%
   credit factor, 2.0% limit) refunding a 2.0% workplace city's tax on home-worked wages keeps
   roughly the 25% the credit never covered — about 0.5% of those wages — under the common
   reading of factor and limit. **Illustrative only; the ordinance controls.** Similar profile:
   [Medina](../municipalities/medina/index.md) (25% factor),
   [Shaker Heights](../municipalities/shaker-heights/index.md) (50%).
4. **Full-credit residence city (credit limit ≥ the workplace rate).** The disallowed credit
   roughly equals the refund: the money largely **moves from the office city to the home city**
   rather than back to the person, with any net coming from rate differences. Examples of the
   profile: [Brecksville](../municipalities/brecksville/index.md),
   [Solon](../municipalities/solon/index.md), [Hudson](../municipalities/hudson/index.md).

To place a specific person: get both cities' rate, credit factor, and credit limit for the
claim year from the [Tax Rates Table](https://www.ritaohio.com/TaxRatesTable) (or the
[municipality sub-bundles](../municipalities/index.md)), read
[Credit for taxes paid](credit-for-taxes-paid.md), and have RITA or a preparer confirm the
computation ([not tax advice](not-tax-advice.md)).
