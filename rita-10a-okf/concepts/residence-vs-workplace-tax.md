# Residence tax vs. workplace tax

The same dollar of wages can be taxed twice, by design:

1. **Workplace municipality** — taxes wages for work physically performed inside its borders.
   Employers must withhold this tax (ORC 718.03(A): withholding applies to qualifying wages
   "earned by the employee in the municipal corporation"), subject to the
   [20-day rule](twenty-day-occasional-entrant.md) for occasional visits.
2. **Residence municipality** — taxes its residents on income wherever earned. It then usually
   grants a [credit for taxes paid](credit-for-taxes-paid.md) to the workplace city — full in
   some cities, partial or capped in others.

Three consequences matter for refunds:

- **Location of work means physical location.** For tax years 2022 and later, a day worked at
  home is a day worked in the *home* municipality (or township), not at the office — see
  [Work-from-home rules by year](work-from-home-rules-by-year.md) for the 2020–2021 exceptions.
- **A workplace refund usually shifts liability rather than erasing it.** If workplace tax is
  refunded via [Form 10A](../procedures/form-10a-wfh-refund.md), the residence city's credit
  shrinks correspondingly — RITA amends residence returns "to disallow any residence tax credit
  taken for amounts refunded," and per RITA's FAQ "you will likely owe that tax, or some portion
  of it, to your residence community." Whether a refund nets anything depends on the residence
  city's credit — see [Credit for taxes paid](credit-for-taxes-paid.md).
- **Days-out refunds from a workplace city are for non-residents of that city.** RITA's Rules &
  Regulations §9(F): "Refunds for days worked out of this municipality are available only to
  non-residents" of it.
