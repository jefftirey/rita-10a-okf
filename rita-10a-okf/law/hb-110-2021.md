# HB 110 (2021), Sections 610.115 and 757.40

Am. Sub. HB 110, 134th General Assembly (the biennial budget bill), signed 2021-07-01;
provisions effective 2021-09-30. **Sections 610.115/610.116** amended and re-enacted
[HB 197's Section 29](hb-197-2020.md); **Section 757.40** addressed employer liability and
refund documentation. **Read the enrolled bill and the LSC analysis** (links below); the
operative language, verbatim:

**Amended Section 29, new division (D)** (enrolled bill, ~p. 2369):

> "On and after January 1, 2021, this section applies only for the purposes of municipal income
> tax withholding under section 718.011 of the Revised Code and for apportioning or situsing the
> employer's net profit under section 718.02 or 718.82 of the Revised Code and not for purposes
> of determining the location at which a nonresident employee's work was completed, services
> were performed or rendered, or activities were conducted for the purpose of determining the
> employee's municipal income tax liability."

The amended rule ran, by its terms, through days worked *"before January, 1, 2022"* [sic, as
enrolled].

**The Legislative Service Commission's description** (Final Bill Analysis, tax portion, p. 447):

> "In effect, the act requires municipalities to approve a nonresident employee's request for a
> refund of taxes withheld on and after January 1, 2021, under the temporary rule, provided the
> income was earned while the employee was not actually working in that municipality for which
> taxes were withheld."

and, same page: *"The act does not provide similar guidance for the treatment of taxes withheld
in 2020."*

**Section 757.40** (enrolled bill, ~p. 2427): division (B) bars assessment of *"any tax,
penalty, or interest"* against employers that withheld to the principal place of work on wages
earned 2020-03-09 through 2021-12-31; division (D) bars tax administrators from requiring refund
documentation beyond an employer statement of days worked at the principal place of work and
that no withheld tax was refunded directly; division (A) declares the changes *"remedial in
nature."*
