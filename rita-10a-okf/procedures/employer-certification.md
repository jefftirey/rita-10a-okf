# Employer certification on Form 10A

## Current rule (TY2024/2025 forms): contact info, no signature

Page 2 of the current Form 10A asks for **"Employer Representative Contact Information"** —
name, title, phone, and company of "a person at your employer who can verify your local
withholding, work locations and refund information. RITA may contact the Employer Representative
to confirm information reported on this Form 10A. **The signature of the Employer Representative
is NOT required.**"

- Required for claim reasons **2–7 and 9** (including the WFH claim).
- Not required for reasons 1 (age exemption), 8 (military spouse), and 10 (overpayment on account).

Practical briefing point: the employee doesn't need to persuade anyone at the employer to sign
anything — they need a payroll or HR contact who would confirm the days-worked facts if RITA calls.

## Why it's lighter than it used to be

The **2020 form** had a true "Employer Certification" in which a representative attested under
signature that the employer over-withheld, that the information was accurate, and that "no
portion of the over-withheld tax has been or will be refunded directly to the employee by the
employer" with no employer-side adjustments made.

**HB 110 (2021), §757.40(D)** then barred tax administrators from requiring refund documentation
beyond an employer statement of the days worked at the principal place of work and that the
withheld tax was not refunded directly. The unsigned contact-info section on today's form
reflects that ceiling.
