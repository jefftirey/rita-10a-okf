# Form 10A step-by-step: the work-from-home refund

Scenario: an employer withheld tax all year for the office municipality, but the employee
actually worked some or all days at home (claim reason 2, "Days Worked From Home"). Applies to
tax years 2022+ as ordinary law; check
[Work-from-home rules by year](../concepts/work-from-home-rules-by-year.md) for older years and
[Open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md) before starting — as of mid-2026 that
means TY2023, 2024, or 2025.

Eligibility gate: days-out refunds from a workplace city are available to **non-residents** of
that city (RITA Rules & Regulations §9(F)) — someone who lives *in* the office city has no
WFH claim against it.

## Steps

1. **Get the right year's form** — forms are year-specific; current and prior years at
   ritaohio.com/individuals/home/formdownloads.
2. **Build the log of days out.** The form requires a "Log of Days Out": municipalities/locations,
   dates, reason, number of days. "Your own worksheet is acceptable" — a calendar export or
   badge/VPN records make it defensible.
3. **Complete the allocation worksheet** (form p. 3). The standard work year is **260 days**
   (52 × 5):
   - Line 1: total available workdays (260) → minus holidays, vacation, sick, personal =
     **days actually worked**.
   - Days worked outside the withholding municipality ÷ days actually worked = the
     out-of-city percentage.
   - Wages = the **larger of W-2 Box 5 or Box 18**. Wages taxable to the office city =
     in-city percentage × wages; over-withholding = W-2 Box 19 minus (taxable wages ×
     the city's rate).
4. **Fill the Claim Summary** (form p. 1): income not taxable, over-withholding claimed, any
   amount to apply to a RITA account instead of refunding, net refund. Refunds of $10 or less
   are not issued.
5. **Provide the employer representative contact** (form p. 2) — required for this claim reason;
   no signature needed. See [Employer certification](employer-certification.md).
6. **Attach documentation** — W-2 and the days-out log at minimum; see the
   [Documentation checklist](documentation-checklist.md).
7. **Sign (under penalties of perjury) and mail** — paper only, to RITA, PO Box 95422,
   Cleveland, OH 44101-0033. One form per W-2, per municipality, per year, per spouse.
8. **Track it**: allow 90 days; status via MyAccount "View History" or 800-860-7482.

## Two consequences to brief alongside the refund

- **Where were those WFH days actually worked?** If in another taxing municipality, that city's
  tax may be due there instead; if in the residence city or a
  [township](../concepts/townships-and-unincorporated.md), the residence rules apply.
- **The residence-side clawback**: RITA amends the residence return to disallow credit taken on
  refunded amounts ([Amended returns](amended-returns.md)); whether the refund nets anything
  depends on the residence city's [credit](../concepts/credit-for-taxes-paid.md). Worked example:
  [Medina resident, Solon office](../scenarios/wfh-2024-medina-resident-solon-office.md).
