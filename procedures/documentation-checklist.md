# Documentation checklist for Form 10A

RITA's rule is blunt: **"No refunds will be issued without the proper documentation indicated by
reason for the claim."** The Tax Administrator may require substantiating documentation
(Rules & Regulations §9). Per claim:

**Every claim:**
- The W-2 for the wages at issue (one Form 10A per W-2, per municipality, per year, per spouse).
- A signed form (taxpayer signs under penalties of perjury; information may be shared with
  resident/workplace tax administrators and the IRS).

**Days worked from home / days out (reasons 2 & 3):**
- **Log of Days Out** — municipalities/locations, dates, reason, day counts; "Your own worksheet
  is acceptable." Calendar exports, badge records, or VPN logs strengthen it.
- The 260-day allocation worksheet (form p. 3) completed.
- [Employer representative contact](employer-certification.md) (no signature).

**Age exemption (reason 1):**
- Proof of birthdate. From TY2024, all municipalities exempt under-18 income uniformly;
  for earlier years the rules varied by municipality — check the municipality node.

**Truck drivers (reason 7):**
- Interstate: none beyond the W-2 (wages taxable only by residence municipality).
- Intrastate: automatic 90% refund from the principal place of work; a refund above 90%
  requires a work-location log.

**Military spouse (reason 8):**
- W-2, Form DD 2058, military spouse ID card, and the service member's LES.

**Overpayment on account (reason 10):**
- No employer involvement needed; the RITA account itself is the record. (If a Form 37 hasn't
  been filed yet, overpayments are claimed there instead — Section B, line 19.)

Also worth knowing: refund review is **suspended** until any required Form 37 is filed; approved
refunds are offset against outstanding balances; interest accrues on overpayments not refunded
within 90 days (ORC 718.12(D)).
