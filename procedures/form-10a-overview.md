# Form 10A: overview

**Form 10A — Application for Municipal Income Tax Refund** — is how anyone claims back
municipal income tax that a RITA member municipality shouldn't keep. Before anything else,
confirm the tax year is still claimable: [Open claim windows](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md).

## The ten claim reasons (TY2025 form)

1. Age exemption (under-18 wages; uniform across all municipalities from TY2024)
2. **Days worked from home** — the remote-work claim; see the
   [step-by-step procedure](form-10a-wfh-refund.md)
3. Other days worked outside the municipality
4. Employer withheld at a rate higher than the municipality's
5. Employer over-withheld residence municipality tax
6. Withheld for the wrong municipality
7. Over-the-road truck driver (interstate: wages taxable only by residence; intrastate:
   automatic 90% refund from the principal place of work, more with a work-location log)
8. Military Spouse Residency Relief Act
9. Other
10. Refund of overpayment already on a RITA account

## Filing mechanics

- **Paper and mail only.** "Form 10A cannot be emailed or electronically filed... Do not fax."
  Mail to: Regional Income Tax Agency, PO Box 95422, Cleveland, OH 44101-0033. RITA's MyAccount
  is for checking status ("View History"), not filing.
- **One Form 10A per W-2, per municipality, per tax year, per spouse** (even on joint accounts).
- Refunds of **$10 or less are not issued**.
- RITA has **90 days** from receiving a complete request to process it; claims are audited first.
- Employer contact info is required for most claim reasons — see
  [Employer certification](employer-certification.md).

## What gets claims denied or stuck

- Filed after the 3-year [statute of limitations](../concepts/statute-of-limitations.md) — denied outright.
- Missing the documentation for the claim reason — "No refunds will be issued without the proper
  documentation" (see [Documentation checklist](documentation-checklist.md)).
- Account not current: review is **suspended** until any required Form 37 (RITA's annual return)
  is filed and processed; approved refunds are **offset** against unpaid balances.
- Tax withheld for a **non-RITA** municipality — RITA cannot refund it
  (see [RITA vs. CCA vs. self-administered](../concepts/rita-vs-cca-vs-self-administered.md)).
- **Stock options and severance** — not refundable; they remain taxable to the employment
  municipality absent a specific exemption.
- A denial (full or partial) arrives as an assessment with appeal instructions
  (ORC 718.19(B)(2); RITA Rules & Regulations §9(C)).

Remember the liability shift: a granted workplace refund usually creates or increases residence
tax — see [Residence vs. workplace tax](../concepts/residence-vs-workplace-tax.md) and
[Amended returns](amended-returns.md).
