# Schaad v. Alder (2024): the 2020 refund question, answered

***Schaad v. Alder*, 2024-Ohio-525, 176 Ohio St.3d 158** — Supreme Court of Ohio, decided
**February 14, 2024**, vote **5-2** (opinion by Justice DeWine; Donnelly, Stewart, Brunner, and
Deters concurring; Chief Justice Kennedy dissenting on Home Rule grounds, Justice Fischer on due
process).

**Facts:** Josh Schaad of Blue Ash had his principal office in Cincinnati and worked from home
from March 2020. Cincinnati refunded his pre-pandemic out-of-city days but refused the WFH days
covered by [HB 197 §29](hb-197-2020.md).

**Holding** (syllabus): the temporary law directing principal-place-of-work municipalities to
collect tax on WFH days "does not violate state or federal Constitutions." Conclusion (¶56):
Section 29 "does not violate the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment... and was a
valid exercise of [the General Assembly's] constitutional authority." The majority distinguished
*intrastate* (city-to-city) from *interstate* taxation for due-process territorial limits, and
held the Home Rule Amendment doesn't stop the General Assembly from granting municipalities
additional taxing authority during an emergency.

**What it means:** Ohio-resident remote workers have **no tax year 2020 WFH refund right**.
RITA had held timely 2020 claims in "SUSPENDED" status pending this litigation; note that RITA's
public pages still carried the suspension language as of 2026-07-14 — an agent should report the
*Schaad* outcome, not the stale page. The 2020 [claim window](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md)
is closed in any case.

**The out-of-state exception — *Morsy v. Dumas*:** Dr. Manal Morsy, a **Pennsylvania** resident
taxed by Cleveland for 2020 days worked entirely in PA, won summary judgment (Cuyahoga C.P.,
2022-09-26); after *Schaad*, Cleveland abandoned its appeal (April 2024) and paid the refund
with interest and costs. Ohio cannot authorize extraterritorial taxation of out-of-state
residents — *Schaad*'s intrastate logic doesn't reach them. Related: *Curcio v. Hufford*
(Toledo/Oregon) was mooted by *Schaad*.

No post-*Schaad* Ohio appellate decisions on pandemic withholding were found through mid-2026;
whether any U.S. Supreme Court review was sought is **unverified**.
