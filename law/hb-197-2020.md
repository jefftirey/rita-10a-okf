# HB 197 (2020), Section 29: the pandemic withholding rule

Am. Sub. HB 197 (133rd General Assembly), Ohio's omnibus COVID-19 emergency bill, was signed
March 27, 2020. Its uncodified **Section 29** (enrolled bill pp. 340–341):

> "Notwithstanding section 718.011 of the Revised Code, and for the purposes of Chapter 718. of
> the Revised Code, during the period of the emergency declared by Executive Order 2020-01D,
> issued on March 9, 2020, and for thirty days after the conclusion of that period, any day on
> which an employee performs personal services at a location, including the employee's home, to
> which the employee is required to report for employment duties because of the declaration
> shall be deemed to be a day performing personal services at the employee's principal place of
> work."

**Effect:** pandemic WFH days counted as office days for municipal income tax — office-city
withholding continued and, for tax year 2020, matched the employee's deemed liability. Suburban
and township residents kept paying the office city's tax while working at their kitchen tables.

**Timeline:**
- In force retroactive to 2020-03-09 (the emergency declaration).
- The emergency was rescinded 2021-06-18 (Executive Order 2021-08D), so the original rule
  expired **2021-07-18** (30 days after) — per the Legislative Service Commission's HB 110
  analysis. [HB 110](hb-110-2021.md) then amended and extended it (withholding-only) through
  2021-12-31.

**Aftermath:** whether 2020 WFH withholding had to be refunded was litigated;
[Schaad v. Alder](schaad-v-alder.md) (2024) upheld Section 29 — no 2020 refunds for Ohio
residents. The [claim window](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md) for 2020 is closed regardless.
