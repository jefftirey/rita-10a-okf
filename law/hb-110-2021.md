# HB 110 (2021): employer relief, employee refunds

Am. Sub. HB 110 (134th General Assembly), the biennial budget bill signed July 1, 2021,
resolved tax year 2021 by splitting [HB 197 §29](hb-197-2020.md) in two:

## What it did (Sections 610.115 / 610.116)

Amended Section 29 to (a) extend the deemed-principal-place-of-work rule for days worked
"in response to the COVID-19 pandemic" through **December 31, 2021**, and (b) add division (D):

> "On and after January 1, 2021, this section applies **only for the purposes of municipal
> income tax withholding**... and **not** for purposes of determining the location at which a
> nonresident employee's work was completed, services were performed or rendered, or activities
> were conducted for the purpose of determining the employee's municipal income tax liability."

The Legislative Service Commission's summary: "In effect, the act requires municipalities to
approve a nonresident employee's request for a refund of taxes withheld on and after
January 1, 2021... provided the income was earned while the employee was not actually working in
that municipality for which taxes were withheld."

## The employer safe harbor (Section 757.40)

- (B): no municipality may assess tax, penalty, or interest against an employer that withheld to
  the principal place of work on wages earned **2020-03-09 through 2021-12-31**.
- (C): the residence municipality treats unrefunded withholding as validly paid.
- (D): tax administrators may **not demand refund documentation beyond** an employer statement of
  days worked at the principal place of work and that no withheld tax was refunded directly —
  the reason today's [employer certification](../procedures/employer-certification.md) is an
  unsigned contact section.
- (A): the changes are "remedial" and apply to all of 2021 (the act's provisions took effect
  2021-09-30, backfilling the gap after the original §29 expired 2021-07-18).

## What it deliberately did not do

Per LSC: "The act does not provide similar guidance for the treatment of taxes withheld in 2020"
— 2020 was left to the courts, and [Schaad v. Alder](schaad-v-alder.md) later closed it. The
amended rule lapsed after 2021-12-31; **tax years 2022+ run on ordinary
[ORC 718](orc-718.md)**. The 2021 [claim window](../concepts/open-claim-windows.md) closed in
April 2025.
